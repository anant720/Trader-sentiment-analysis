UI/UX Master Specification: MIT ADT Student Hub
This document acts as the definitive design prompt and UX manual for the application. It describes the "Soul" of the app—how it looks, how it moves, and how it feels to the student.
🎨 1. Visual Identity & "Modern Native" Feel
Aesthetic: Modern Native (Instagram-style). High-contrast, clean, and extremely professional. No over-the-top blurs or glowing gradients. It should feel like a multi-million dollar production app.
Color Palette:
Light Mode: Pure White (#FFFFFF) background with Light Grey (#F0F2f5) accents.
Dark Mode: Deep Black (#000000) background with Charcoal (#1C1C1E) card surfaces.
Accent: A single, professional primary color (e.g., University Blue #0056B3) for buttons and active states.
Typography: Using System Fonts (San Francisco for iOS / Roboto for Android) to ensure the app feels like a native part of the phone.
📸 2. The Faculty Explore Grid (The "Real Feed")
Layout: High-density Grid.
Tile Design:
Realistic Branding: Tiles will look like professional "Profile Cards." Clean, sharp borders (radius: 12px) and a very subtle drop shadow to show depth.
The "Photo" Area: A solid, color-blocked square at the top of the tile containing the Department Name (e.g., "CSE") in bold, white, professional sans-serif text.
Information: Faculty Name and a clean "Cabin" badge (e.g., "ROOM S305") on a neutral background below the color block.
Interactions: "Nippy" and fast. Small scale-down effect on press to give tactile feedback.
🛠️ 3. Navigation & Header UX
The Header (Sticky):
Search: A flat, white/black search bar with a thin grey border.
Quick Filters: Solid "Chiclets" (chips). Selected chips turn to the Primary Accent color with white text.
Navigation (Bottom Bar):
Standard, non-transparent white/black bar at the base.
High-quality, thin-stroke Outline Icons for unselected states; Solid Icons for active states—exactly like Instagram.
📋 4. Detailed Component Experience
Faculty Detail Card:
A slide-up "Bottom Sheet" (Standard mobile interaction).
Clean, white/black background with high-contrast text.
Big, bold Action Button: [Report an Error].
Productivity (Tasks):
Clean, list-based UI with native "Checkboxes."
Language Switcher: A standard "Picker" or a "Sheet" that pops up from the bottom.
✨ 5. Native Motion & Performance
Transition: Use "Standard Easing." No "Elastic" or "Bouncing" animations. Everything should feel lightning-fast and responsive.
Image Handling: Use standard aspect ratios (1:1 or 4:5) for all posters/tiles to maintain a grid that looks organized and "Real."
🚀 6. The "Social" Vision UX
Future Elements:
Messaging: Circular chat bubbles at the top of the Hub tab.
Student Feed: A scrolled vertical feed of campus stories that look like high-quality digital posters.
Unique IDs: Each student's profile features a "QR Student Badge" for quick sharing and admin support.
📢 The "Developer Handshake" (About Section)
A very personal, high-quality profile section featuring:
The Identity: Explicitly states: "This is a personal student-led project. Created by students, for students." (Not an official university app).
Professional Growth: A timeline showing your journey from "Student" to the developer of this hub.
Direct Links: Glowing buttons for GitHub and LinkedIn.
